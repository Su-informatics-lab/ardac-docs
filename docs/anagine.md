# Anagine

## Overview

Anagine is an ARDaC analysis workspace designed to help researchers explore approved datasets, prepare analysis-ready cohorts, and run reproducible workflows in a secure environment. It connects data discovery, controlled access, and computational analysis so teams can move from question to result with fewer manual handoffs.

## What You Can Do

Use Anagine to review available project data, create analysis cohorts, launch notebooks or workflow tools, and organize outputs for downstream review. Depending on your permissions, you may also connect to approved data files, reference metadata from the ARDaC portal, and collaborate with other authorized users.

## Access Requirements

Access to Anagine is based on your ARDaC account, institutional role, project membership, and data-use approvals. Some datasets or tools may require additional authorization before they are visible or usable in the workspace.

## Getting Started

Sign in with your ARDaC credentials and open the Anagine workspace from the portal or tools page. Select an approved project, confirm that the expected datasets are available, and choose the analysis environment that matches your task.

## Recommended Workflow

Start by defining the research question and selecting the relevant cohort or files. Document any filters, transformations, or assumptions before running analysis. Save scripts, notebooks, workflow configuration, and result summaries in a project location that can be reviewed by the team.

## Support

For help with workspace access, missing datasets, workflow errors, or environment configuration, contact the ARDaC support team with your project name, workspace name, and a short description of the issue.
